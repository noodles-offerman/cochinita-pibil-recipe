## Cochinita Pibil (Quick Recipe)

Cochinita Pibil is a traditional dish from Yucatán, Mexico.
It’s slow-cooked pork marinated in achiote, orange juice, and spices.

For more about Yucatán food, see [this article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucatecan_cuisine).

### Ingredients
- 1 kg pork shoulder, cut into large chunks
- 3 tbsp achiote paste
- 1 cup orange juice (or half orange + half lime)
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp oregano
- 1 red onion, sliced
- Corn tortillas

## Instructions
1. Blend the achiote paste, orange juice, garlic, salt, and oregano to make the marinade.
2. Place the pork in a bowl or bag and cover with the marinade.
3. Cook slowly (oven at 150 °C or slow cooker on low) for 3–4 hours until the pork is tender and falls apart.
4. Shred the pork and mix with the remaining juices.
5. Serve with pickled red onion and warm tortillas.

## Tips for serving

- Serve with **pickled red onion** and **warm tortillas**.
